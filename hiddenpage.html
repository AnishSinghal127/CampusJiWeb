Rationale for Website Structure and Navigation

A stakeholder analysis was conducted to help structure the website in accordance with the stakeholder's needs.
A Google form survey was floated across batches of IIM Rohtak for the same.
The questionnaire and responses can be accessed here.

The following results were obtained from the google form which helped us in structuring the final website:

No. Result Action Taken Rationale
1. A lack of a consolidated place for essential numbers and tracking for solving hostel-level issues emerged as the foremost issues of urgency. Service tab placed immediately after the homepage. Stakeholders are our priority, and to ensure that they get their work done in the least possible time, we have formatted the webpages in order of urgency. Both grievance redressal portals and contact repository are included in the service section of the website

Business Model Canvas

Key Partners
  • University administration (for official data & approvals)
  • Student clubs & societies (content and event updates)
  • IT department / hosting provider
  • Campus services (mess, hostel office, parcel desk)
  • Local businesses for possible ads or sponsorships
Key Activities
  • Maintaining & updating website features (contacts, menus, parcel alerts, grievance reporting, lost & found, events)
  • Content moderation
  • User support & feedback collection
  • Marketing to students & onboarding new users
  • Data security & uptime monitoring
Key Resources
  • Web development & design team
  • Hosting infrastructure & domain
  • Content management system
  • Data from university departments
  • Branding & marketing assets
Value Proposition
  • For Students: A one-stop hub for campus life, reducing confusion and missed information
  • For University: A structured communication channel to reach students effectively
  • For Clubs/Societies: A platform to promote events and recruit members
Customer Relationships
  • Self-service platform with intuitive navigation
  • Community-driven (students can submit events/grievances)
  • Feedback loops via surveys & suggestion boxes
  • Occasional promotional campaigns to boost engagement
Channels
  • CampusJi website (primary)
  • QR codes on campus notice boards
  • University email newsletters
  • Social media integration (Instagram, WhatsApp groups, LinkedIn, etc.)
Customer Segments
  • Primary: University students (UG & PG)
  • Secondary: University staff, faculty, and administrative offices
  • Tertiary: Prospective students & parents (for campus life preview)
Cost Structure
  • Web hosting & maintenance
  • Domain registration
  • Developer salaries / stipends
  • Marketing & promotions
  • Content creation & graphic design
Revenue Streams
  • University funding or budget allocation
  • Sponsorships from local businesses
  • Event promotions
  • Premium listing for clubs/societies (if applicable)
  • Minimal, non-intrusive ads
